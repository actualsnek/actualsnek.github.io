Essential Reading List

Epic Origins
  1. Homer — The Odyssey (Wilson)
  2. Hesiod — Theogony (West or Most)
  3. Homer — The Iliad (Wilson or Alexander)
  4. Apollodorus — The Library (Hard)
Classical Expression

Add to the above:

  1. Homeric Hymns (West or Cashford)
  2. Aeschylus — Oresteia (Sommerstein)
  3. Sophocles — Oedipus Rex, Antigone (Grene/Lattimore)
  4. Euripides — Medea, Bacchae, Hippolytus (Kovacs)
  5. Ovid — Metamorphoses (Melville or Martin)
Additional Texts

Add to the above:

  1. Apollonius — Argonautica (Hunter or Race)
  2. Remaining tragedies
  3. Pindar — Selected Odes (Race)

Beyond the Canon

If Interested

The full breadth of surviving Greek & Roman mythological literature

Epic Poetry
  • Iliad & Odyssey Homer
  • Theogony & Works and Days Hesiod
  • Argonautica Apollonius of Rhodes
  • Shield of Heracles Hesiod (attrib.)
  • Posthomerica Quintus of Smyrna The fall of Troy, continuation of the Iliad
  • The Taking of Ilios (Iliupersis) Arctinus of Miletus Lost — survives only in summary (Epic Cycle)
  • Telegony Eugammon of Cyrene Sequel to the Odyssey — Epic Cycle fragment
  • Dionysiaca Nonnus of Panopolis 48 books on Dionysus — late antique epic
Hymns & Lyric Poetry
  • Homeric Hymns Anonymous
  • Olympian & Pythian Odes Pindar
  • Nemean & Isthmian Odes Pindar
  • Odes (fragments) Bacchylides
  • Odes (fragments) Simonides
  • Orphic Hymns Anonymous 87 hymns to gods — mystery cult tradition
  • Callimachus — Hymns & Aetia Callimachus Alexandrian mythology in epigrammatic style
Tragedy
  • Oresteia, Prometheus Bound, Seven Against Thebes, Persians, Suppliants Aeschylus
  • Oedipus Rex, Antigone, Electra, Ajax, Philoctetes, Trachiniae, Oedipus at Colonus Sophocles
  • Medea, Hippolytus, Bacchae, Alcestis, Hecuba, Trojan Women, Ion, Iphigenia at Aulis & Tauris, Orestes, Helen, Andromache, Electra, Heracles, Cyclops, Rhesus, Phoenician Women, Suppliant Women Euripides
  • Hercules Furens, Medea, Agamemnon, Thyestes, Oedipus, Troades, Phaedra Seneca Roman tragedies on Greek myths
Mythology Compendia & Reference
  • Bibliotheca (The Library) Apollodorus / Pseudo-Apollodorus
  • Fabulae (Genealogiae) Hyginus 277 myth summaries — invaluable reference
  • Astronomica (Poetica Astronomica) Hyginus Myths behind the constellations
  • Catasterismi Eratosthenes (attrib.) Myths of stellar catasterisms
  • Mythologiae Fulgentius Late antique allegorical myths
  • Imagines (Eikones) Philostratus the Elder Ekphrasis of mythological paintings
Roman Epic & Poetry
  • Metamorphoses Ovid
  • Aeneid Virgil Foundation of Rome — Trojan cycle continuation
  • Heroides, Amores, Ars Amatoria, Fasti Ovid Letters of mythological heroines; Roman calendar myths
  • Thebaid Statius War of the Seven Against Thebes
  • Achilleid Statius Achilles' youth — unfinished
  • Punica Silius Italicus Rich in mythological episodes
  • De Raptu Proserpinae Claudian Abduction of Persephone — late antique
Geographical & Travel Literature
  • Description of Greece (Periegesis) Pausanias Regional myths tied to monuments — 10 books
  • Library of History Diodorus Siculus Books I–V especially: Olympians, heroes, theogonies
  • Geographica Strabo Scattered mythological commentary throughout
Philosophy & Allegorical Interpretation
  • Symposium, Phaedrus, Timaeus, Protagoras Plato Original mythological creation (Atlantis, Er, Eros)
  • De Natura Deorum Cicero Philosophical critique of traditional mythology
  • Moralia — On Isis and Osiris, On the Face of the Moon Plutarch Syncretism and allegorical myth reading
  • Allegories of Homer Heraclitus (Rhetor) Stoic allegorical reading of the Iliad & Odyssey
  • On the Gods and the World Sallustius Neoplatonic interpretation of myth
Greek Romance & Prose Fiction
  • Daphnis and Chloe Longus Pastoral romance with Dionysus & Eros
  • Leucippe and Clitophon Achilles Tatius
  • True History Lucian of Samosata Parody of mythological voyages
  • Dialogues of the Gods & of the Dead Lucian of Samosata Witty satirical reworkings of myth
  • The Golden Ass (Metamorphoses) Apuleius Contains Cupid & Psyche — only full telling
Mythography & Scholia
  • Mythographi Vaticani I, II, III Anonymous (Medieval compilations) Medieval collections preserving lost sources
  • Scholia on Homer Various Ancient commentaries — rich mythological variants
  • Scholia on Pindar Various
  • Scholia on Apollonius of Rhodes Various
  • Etymologicum Magnum Anonymous Byzantine Byzantine lexicon with mythological entries
  • Suda Anonymous Byzantine 10th-c. encyclopedia with mythological fragments
Lost Works (Known from Fragments)
  • Ehoiai (Catalogue of Women) Hesiod Heroic genealogies — large fragments survive
  • Epic Cycle (Cypria, Aethiopis, Little Iliad, Nostoi) Various Proclus summaries + Photius survive
  • Satyr Plays (fragments) Aeschylus, Sophocles, Euripides Comic mythological drama — mostly lost
  • Phoronis, Titanomachy, Naupactia Various (archaic) Archaic epics — only fragments remain
  • Orphic Theogonies (Rhapsodies) Orphic tradition Alternative theogonic tradition — reconstructed
Late Antique & Byzantine
  • Argonautica Orphica Anonymous Orphic retelling of the Jason myth
  • Colluthus — The Rape of Helen Colluthus
  • Tryphiodorus — The Fall of Troy Tryphiodorus
  • Musaeus — Hero and Leander Musaeus Grammaticus Beautiful short epic — 5th/6th c. AD
  • Mythologies Fulgentius Christianizing allegory of pagan myth

Works marked as "lost" survive only in fragments, summaries, or quotations by later authors. Loeb Classical Library and Budé series contain most of the fragmentary material.
