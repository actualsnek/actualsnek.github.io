II

Greek Tragedy

Aeschylus · Sophocles · Euripides

The three great tragedians whose works define Western drama, philosophy, and our understanding of fate.

Aeschylus c. 525–456 BC

Father of tragedy. Invented dramatic dialogue. Won the City Dionysia 13 times. Only 7 of his 70–90 plays survive.

Sophocles c. 497–406 BC

Added the third actor. Won 24 victories at the Dionysia — never came last. Only 7 of his 120+ plays survive.

Euripides c. 480–406 BC

The most modern of the three. Psychological realism, questioning of the gods, strong female characters. 18 plays survive.
