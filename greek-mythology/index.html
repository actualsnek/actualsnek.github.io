Prima Elementa — The First Elements

Neophytus Es? — Art Thou a Newcomer?

Before the primaries, begin here

The primary texts in this guide are the real thing — ancient epic poetry, tragedy, and myth as the Greeks actually wrote it. But if you have never encountered Greek myth before, diving straight into Homer can feel like jumping into the deep end. These books give you the entire mythological world first, so that when you reach the primaries, everything clicks.

Step I
Mythology
Edith Hamilton
The single best introduction. Covers every major god and hero. Start here.
Step II
Mythos
Stephen Fry
Thorough retelling of the creation myths and Olympians.
Step III
The Greek Myths
Robert Graves
Every myth and variant, exhaustively footnoted. Use as a reference companion once reading primaries.
Step IV
The Complete World of Greek Mythology
Richard Buxton
Explains the social and geographical context behind the myths. Superb illustrations.
Then Read
The Odyssey
Homer (Wilson trans.)
Once you know the mythological world, start here. The most readable primary text — your gateway into the canon.

Essential Reading List

Epic Origins
  1. Homer — The Odyssey (Wilson)
  2. Hesiod — Theogony (West or Most)
  3. Homer — The Iliad (Wilson or Alexander)
  4. Apollodorus — The Library (Hard)
Classical Expression

Add to the above:

  1. Homeric Hymns (West or Cashford)
  2. Aeschylus — Oresteia (Sommerstein)
  3. Sophocles — Oedipus Rex, Antigone (Grene / Lattimore)
  4. Euripides — Medea, Bacchae, Hippolytus (Kovacs)
  5. Ovid — Metamorphoses (Melville or Martin)
Additional Texts

Add to the above:

  1. Apollonius — Argonautica (Hunter or Race)
  2. Remaining tragedies
  3. Pindar — Selected Odes (Race)
